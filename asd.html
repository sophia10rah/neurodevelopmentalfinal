Autism Spectrum Disorder (ASD)

Key Facts & Research

Core Features of ASD

Prevalence

  • 1 in 36 children
  • 1 in 25 males
  • 1 in 100 females

Diagnosis

Typically diagnosed by age 3, based on behavioral observations.

Core Symptoms

  • Social communication challenges
  • Restricted/repetitive behaviors

Early Risk Factors

Infant Siblings (ASIBs)

  • 18-20x more likely to be diagnosed
  • Broader Autism Phenotype (BAP)

Brain Changes in Infancy

  • Cortical Hyperexpansion (6-12 months)
  • MRI Predictors (Hazlett et al., 2017)

Brain Development in ASD

Childhood to Adolescence

  • Surface Area: Abnormal growth in social brain regions
  • Thickness: Less distinct compared to neurotypical peers
  • Connectivity Issues: Poor synchronization between brain regions

Causes of ASD

Genetic Factors

  • Strong Familial Link
  • 100s of Candidate Genes
  • Epigenetic Influences

Environmental Factors

  • Pesticide exposure (1st trimester)
  • No link to vaccines

Neurobiology & Treatment Insights

Neurotransmitters

  • Oxytocin: Lower levels linked to social deficits
  • Serotonin: Elevated in 25% of cases

Gut-Brain Connection

  • Gut Microbiome research
  • Key Players: GABA, BDNF

Why ASD Is Linked to the Frontal Lobe

Neurochemical Disruption

  • Abnormal NAA/Cr (neuronal integrity) ratios in frontal white matter
  • Altered Cho/Cr (membrane turnover) ratios
  • Indicates metabolic dysfunction in frontal regions

Core Symptom Connection

  • Governs executive functions (planning, flexibility)
  • Controls emotion recognition
  • Both functions commonly impaired in ASD

Neuroimaging Validation

  • Direct link between frontal metabolite changes and ASD symptoms
  • Connects metabolic alterations to cognitive deficits
  • Correlates with social impairment in ASD

Margari, L., De Giacomo, A., Craig, F., Palumbi, R., Peschechera, A., Margari, M., Picardi, F., Caldarola, M., Maghenzani, M. A., & Dicuonzo, F. (2018). Frontal lobe metabolic alterations in autism spectrum disorder: a 1H-magnetic resonance spectroscopy study. Neuropsychiatric disease and treatment, 14, 1871–1876. https://doi.org/10.2147/NDT.S165375

Summary

  • Early brain overgrowth and lifelong challenges
  • Complex causes: Genetics, environment, and epigenetics
  • Promising research in early detection and treatment

ASD Knowledge Quiz

1. What is the current estimated prevalence of ASD in children?

A) 1 in 100
B) 1 in 36
C) 1 in 10
D) 1 in 500
The CDC's 2023 data shows 1 in 36 children are diagnosed with ASD, with higher rates in males.

2. Infant siblings of children with ASD (ASIBs) are ___ times more likely to be diagnosed with ASD.

A) 2–3x
B) 18–20x
C) 5–10x
D) 50x
ASIBs have significantly higher genetic risk due to shared familial factors.

3. Which brain change is observed in some infants later diagnosed with ASD?

A) Shrinkage of the hippocampus
B) Reduced amygdala size
C) Hyperexpansion of cortical surface area (6–12 months)
D) Delayed myelination
Hazlett et al. (2017) linked early brain overgrowth to later social deficits.

4. Which environmental factor has research linked to increased ASD risk?

A) Thimerosal in vaccines
B) First-trimester pesticide exposure
C) Screen time before age 2
D) Processed sugar consumption
Studies suggest pesticides (not vaccines) may elevate risk, especially without folic acid.

5. Which neurotransmitter is linked to social deficits in ASD and may improve symptoms when supplemented?

A) Dopamine
B) Serotonin
C) Oxytocin
D) GABA
Oxytocin enhances social bonding, and trials show it may improve eye contact in ASD.
