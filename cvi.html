Cerebral Visual Impairment (CVI)

Key Facts & Research

Core Features of CVI

Definition

A brain-based visual impairment caused by damage to the visual pathways (occipital lobe, optic radiations) not the eyes.

Prevalence

Leading cause of childhood visual impairment in developed countries.

Diagnosis

Often missed—requires specialized assessment (e.g., functional vision tests).

Brain Mechanisms

Occipital Lobe Damage

  • Disrupts processing of visual signals
  • Caused by prenatal hypoxia, stroke, or TBI

Two Subtypes

  • Dorsal Stream Dysfunction: Spatial awareness issues
  • Ventral Stream Dysfunction: Face/object recognition problems

Common Symptoms

Visual Preferences

  • Needs high-contrast colors
  • Avoids complex scenes
  • Recognizes objects only when moving

Challenges

  • Struggles with faces
  • Difficulty with 3D shapes
  • Uses touch/sound to navigate

Causes & Risk Factors

Prenatal/Perinatal

  • Prematurity
  • Lack of oxygen (HIE)
  • Infections

Genetic & Comorbidities

  • ALDH1A3 and POMT1 gene mutations
  • Cerebral Palsy
  • Epilepsy
  • Global delays

Interventions & Support

Environmental Adaptations

  • Simple, uncluttered visuals
  • Extra processing time
  • High-contrast materials

Therapies

  • Vision rehabilitation
  • Sensory substitution
  • Scanning strategies

Tech Tools

  • High-contrast apps
  • Audiobooks
  • Assistive technology

Key Takeaways

  • CVI is invisible—kids may see "normally" but struggle to understand visuals
  • Early intervention improves outcomes (e.g., literacy, independence)
  • CVI can improve with targeted therapy (unlike degenerative eye conditions)
  • Visual perception varies significantly from typical vision

CVI Knowledge Quiz

1. CVI is caused by damage to the:

A) Retina
B) Occipital lobe
C) Cornea
D) Optic nerve
CVI stems from brain (not eye) damage—often in the visual cortex.

2. True or False: Kids with CVI always have severe intellectual disabilities.

True
False
CVI severity varies; many have typical cognition but need visual adaptations.

3. Which is a hallmark sign of CVI?

A) Nearsightedness
B) Color blindness
C) Difficulty recognizing faces
D) Double vision
Ventral stream dysfunction impairs facial recognition.

4. What environmental change helps kids with CVI?

A) Dim lighting
B) High-contrast toys
C) Patterned wallpaper
D) Fast-moving cartoons
Bold colors (e.g., red/yellow) are easier to process.

5. Which gene is linked to some CVI cases?

A) MECP2
B) FMR1
C) ALDH1A3
D) HTT
ALDH1A3 gene mutations have been linked to some cases of CVI.
